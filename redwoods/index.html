Redwood Forest Conservation

Ancient, Towering and Rare

Good Shepherd promotes redwood forest conservation an essential practice for the well-being of one of the world's redwood forests that contain trees that live over 2,000 years, support a vast number of plants, animals and fungi species. Redwood forests in Northern California, and their freshwater lagoons, are being logged areas, and are threatened by climate change and protecting old growth stands, restoring logged areas, and managing the forests sustainably. The redwood forest is also home to a variety of insects and salamanders. These complex forest layers for food and shelter.
